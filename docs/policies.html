Library policies & governance

These guidelines ensure every edition published by the Waypoint Institute Library is responsibly sourced, preserved, and governed with academic rigor.

Collection development & rights

We focus on public-domain and openly licensed texts suitable for research and teaching. Every item is vetted for provenance, rights status, and editorial quality before publication.

  • Preference for texts with established scholarly value or instructional demand.
  • Source materials must be public domain or released under an open license that permits redistribution.
  • Digital assets over 25 MB are mirrored to Zenodo or the Internet Archive for redundancy.

Rights & takedown

If you believe a work infringes copyright or includes sensitive material, email library@waypoint.institute with the record ID and supporting documentation. We review requests within five business days.

  • We temporarily hide contested items during review.
  • We communicate outcomes to requesters and update public metadata when removals occur.
  • Valid takedowns prompt updates to the preservation manifest and search index.

Preservation plan

All editions are stored in Git, exported to archival formats (HTML, EPUB, PDF), and synchronized to off-site storage with checksum verification every quarter.

  • We maintain JSON-LD records for machine-readable provenance.
  • Checksums for downloadable assets ship alongside the static site.
  • Nightly GitHub Actions verify links, metadata, and build artifacts.
